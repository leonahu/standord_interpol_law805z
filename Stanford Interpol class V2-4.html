Policy Practicum: Rethinking INTERPOL's Governance Model

Designing a Policy Framework to Facilitate Information Exchange between INTERPOL and Private Sector 
 

LAW-805Z 
SUBMIT FORM TO SIGNUP 

Advances in internet technologies create new challenges for law enforcement. Effective law enforcement operations increasingly depends on cooperation and information exchange with private sector. But law enforcement authorities can face difficulties in effectively accessing and utilizing information from private sector actors. INTERPOL—the world’s largest international police organization— strives to innovate and adequately respond to the evolving law enforcement environment. It seeks to review its policies on collecting, handling, and sharing data from private sector sources, such as tech companies. 

Students in this practicum will work with INTERPOL to design an innovative policy framework. The framework aims to permit appropriate law enforcement access to data and digital evidence in private sector hands while simultaneously protecting personal privacy. The Stanford team will work directly with INTERPOL to enhance its role fighting cyber-crime, terrorism, and other forms of transnational crime.

This practicum takes place for two quarters (Fall and Winter), followed by a study trip to INTERPOL headquarters in Lyon over the Spring Break for policy briefings. Internship opportunities are independently available at the Office of Legal Affairs, INTERPOL General Secretariat in Lyon, France and/or at INTERPOL Global Complex for Innovation in Singapore.
